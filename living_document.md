If this gap is not closed, we face a future of **"High-Velocity Error"**: a state where decisions are made with unprecedented speed and scale, but are fundamentally unmoored from human values, purpose, and the ability to detect the "black swan" events that fall outside the algorithmic training set. The path forward requires treating decision-making not as a byproduct of technology, but as a core, human-centric discipline that must be intentionally designed to include both machine speed and human accountability.
