# FOUNDATIONAL HYPOTHESES

## Hypothesis 1: The Shift from Information Acquisition to Cognitive Defense

**Description:** The primary psychological stressor has shifted from the difficulty of *not knowing* to the functional inability/refusal to *process* overwhelming noise. This marks a fundamental transition in the human cognitive role: moving from the **Collector** (valuing the acquisition of data) to the **Filterer/Pruner** (valuing the management of exclusion). 

This involves a critical distinction between two failure modes:
1.  **Systemic Overload (The Input):** The structural architecture of digital tools that forces high-frequency, micro-context-switching.
2.  **Cognitive Fatigue (The Cost):** The biological depletion of executive function and the emergence of defensive strategies (e.g., intentional neglect of alerts, sensory withdrawal).

**Evidence required for validation:** Increasing correlation between digital noise levels and measurable declines in compliance/security; linguistic shifts toward 'digital hygiene'; adoption of AI synthesis tools as a way to outsource curation burden.

**Evidence that would weaken it:** Evidence that human attention remains highly elastic or that information volume is decreasing.

## Hypothesis 3: The Structural Decoupling of Truth

**Description:** The "Enclosure of Knowledge" is driving a fundamental bifurcation of reality. As foundational/scientific data is increasingly shielded behind automated denials and institutional moats, a structural decoupling occurs: 
1. **The Protected Layer:** A high-fidelity, credentialed reality accessible only to those with the 'keys' (subscriptions, permissions).
2. **The Exposed Layer:** An increasingly hollowed-out public discourse that must attempt to interpret reality using only unverified, secondary, or inaccessible signals.

This shift creates a new psychological state of **Epistemic Frustration**, where the act of searching moves from *discovery* to *negotiation with gates*, ultimately leading to an alienation from the official layers of reality.

**Evidence required for validation:** Increasing frequency of paywalls/access denials in foundational repositories; growing divergence between academic consensus and public-facing digital discourse; linguistic shifts toward 'gatekeeping' as a technical/algorithmic rather than purely social phenomenon.

**Evidence that would weaken it:** Evidence of a resurgence in Open Access models that successfully reintegrate foundational data into the public commons.
